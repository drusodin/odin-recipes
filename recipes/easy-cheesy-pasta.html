Easy Cheesy Pasta

Description

This is one of my go-to pasta recipes. It's quick, easy to make, and tastes absolutely fire. There's only a few ingredients, stuff many Americans would have laying around. I recommend going for a quality cheese, but no shame if all you have is that powdery Kraft stuff in the shaker bottle. It will get the job done!

Ingredients

  • 90 g Dried pasta (Spaghetti recommended)
  • 1/2 Cup Whole Milk or Heavy Whipping Cream
  • As much Parmesan cheese as your heart desires
  • 14 g (1 tbsp) Salted Butter
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Boil and salt your a pot of water. I don't have measurements for this, you can freestyle it. There should be enough water that your pasta fits. Make it pretty salty. Not like ocean salty but maybe sweat salty...
  2. Add your pasta. Let it cook until it's got a bit of chew to it (around 10 min). AL dente as they say.
  3. Around the 5 min mark, start to heat milk/cream and butter over medium heat in a separate pot. Don't let this get too hot too fast or it will scorch. Treat it gentle, like you love it.
  4. Grate your parmesan into the sauce. Or pour straight in if you're going pre-grated. The cheese will thicken the milk sauce.
  5. Strain your pasta. Add strained pasta to the sauce. Mix it up until desired consistency. Add more cream/milk if needed. Remember it will thicken when it cools so it leave it a bit more watery than you'd like it when served.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Add more parmesan over the top.
